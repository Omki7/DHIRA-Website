Product Strategy · Gap Analysis · 05 June 2026

Where Akashic stops short of its own promise.

Akashic today is a genuinely impressive intelligence surface — the Enterprise Spine, the persona lenses, the cross-pillar composite, the reverse-ETL action composer and the citation-backed trust layer are all real and well-built. This document is not a critique of that work. It is a product-envisioner's map of the gaps between what the system shows and what an executive actually needs to run the firm — across workflows, functions, and features — with a proposed solution for each, and a sequencing recommendation at the end.

The central finding: Akashic is excellent at telling you what is true right now, and good at letting you fire a single action — but it does not yet close the loop, see forward in time, scale past three seats, let people decide together, or answer a question you actually type. Those are the themes below.

A Scorecard — strong foundations, missing connective tissue

What is genuinely strong today

  • ✓The Enterprise Spine concept — a single connected graph that fuses Client→Project→Employee→Financial signals into one composite risk.
  • ✓Persona-aware home — pulse tiles, AI brief, rails, hero modules and feed priority all re-shape per role.
  • ✓Reverse-ETL action composer — every signal is one click from a write-back to Salesforce / SAP / Tally / Darwinbox / Zoho, with an approval gate.
  • ✓Trust layer for reading — confidence scores, source citations, and a document-validation queue behind every number.
  • ✓Cross-persona handoff — a signal can be routed to a colleague with context preserved.

What is structurally missing

  • ✕The loop never closes — actions are fire-and-forget; nothing tracks the outcome or lets the AI learn.
  • ✕The headline feature is a façade — "Ask Akashic" can't answer a typed question; scenarios are three fixed buttons.
  • ✕No time axis — everything is a snapshot of today; no horizon, no scheduled brief delivery.
  • ✕Three seats, not an org — one hardcoded user per role; no scoped/functional roles, no delegation.
  • ✕No collaboration, no control plane, no mobile — executives decide alone, at a desk, against hardcoded thresholds.
Severity: Critical blocks the core value proposition High limits real-world adoption Medium depth & polish

01 The intelligence loop never closes Gaps 1–3 · the biggest conceptual hole

Akashic's premise is "every figure one click from action." It delivers the click — but an action composer that ends at "synced to systems" is only half a loop. Once an invoice is raised or a retention package approved, the system forgets it happened. There is no record of whether the decision worked, no learning signal back into the confidence models, and no way to act on a recommendation as a whole rather than as a pile of disconnected buttons. This is the single highest-leverage area to fix.

G1
Actions are fire-and-forget — there is no outcome tracking or decision journal
Criticalworkfloweffort · Mtouches: actions.js · Approvals

Today

The composer ends at a sync animation and a toast. The audit log records that an action fired, but nothing records what happened next. Did Northwind pay after the dunning notice? Did Vikram accept the hike? Did the re-forecast prove accurate? The executive who decided has no place to see the consequence of their decision, and no nudge to follow up.

Proposed solution

Add a Decision Journal — every action carries a lifecycle past "synced": in-effect → awaiting-outcome → resolved (worked / didn't).

  • A "My Decisions" view: open commitments, their due-by, and realized result.
  • Auto-follow-up tasks ("check Northwind 7 days after notice").
  • Outcome capture writes back to the entity timeline, not just an audit row.
Evidence: actions.js submit() pushes to a queue with status:'pending'; simulateSync() animates and ends — no post-completion state exists. Approvals.html audit log is append-only fact, not outcome.
G2
The AI never learns — confidence scores are static and never recalibrate
Criticalfunctioneffort · Ltouches: data model · Assistant

Today

Every prediction ships with a confidence (renewal 54% · 0.84 conf, attrition 88%, composite 0.91). But these are authored constants. When Meridian does or doesn't renew, nothing feeds back. A system that calls itself "intelligence" but cannot improve from its own track record is a dashboard with a personality.

Proposed solution

Close the model loop using G1's outcome data:

  • Track prediction vs. realized outcome per signal type; surface model accuracy ("renewal model: 81% hit rate, last 40 calls").
  • Let executives correct/override a signal — captured as training feedback.
  • Show a calibration badge next to confidence so users know which models to trust.
Evidence: confidence values are literals in Home.html feed cards (0.84), Assistant.html evidence rows, and data.js (conf:0.71). No accuracy/feedback store exists.
G3
Recommendations aren't executable — there are no plans or playbooks, only single buttons
Highworkfloweffort · Mtouches: Assistant · actions.js

Today

Akashic gives beautifully specific multi-step advice — "assign Priya to open a renewal SOW, deploy a shadow for Vikram, recover the ₹48 L unbilled before the renewal conversation." But the executive must then manually fire three separate composer actions and remember the sequence. The recommendation and the execution are disconnected.

Proposed solution

Introduce Playbooks — a recommendation becomes a bundled, ordered action plan:

  • "Accept plan" fans out into the constituent composer actions, each pre-filled, each with its own approver.
  • Progress tracked as one workstream ("Meridian Save Plan · 2 of 3 done").
  • A library of reusable playbooks (renewal-at-risk, key-person exit, margin recovery).
Evidence: Assistant.html recommend field is prose; the composite card on Home.html offers 4 unlinked buttons. No multi-action bundle primitive in actions.js.

02 The headline feature is a façade Gaps 4–5 · "Ask Akashic" promises more than it does

The product is named for asking Akashic. It sits second in every sidebar and is the call-to-action on the home brief. Yet it cannot currently answer a question a user types, and the scenario engine offers exactly three pre-written outcomes. This is the widest promise-vs-reality gap in the product, and the one a demo-savvy buyer will probe first.

G4
Ask Akashic can't answer a typed question — the conversation is hardcoded
Criticalfunctioneffort · M–Ltouches: Assistant.html

Today

The assistant renders a fixed two-turn script about Meridian. Typing anything and pressing "Ask ›" fires AK.toast('Query sent') and clears the box — no answer ever appears. The suggestion chips do the same. The entire grounded-Q&A experience is a stage set.

Proposed solution

Make it actually respond, grounded in the live AKData model:

  • Wire to a real completion endpoint with the data model as context; keep the evidence-citation format that already looks excellent.
  • Even without an LLM: a templated NL→query layer over AKData can answer the top ~20 executive questions deterministically.
  • Persist conversation history; deep-link answers to the entity 360s.
Evidence: Assistant.html — const convo=[…] is literal; sendQ() only toasts; addUser() sets the input value and toasts "thinking" but never appends a turn.
G5
Scenario simulation is three fixed buttons, not a what-if model
Highfunctioneffort · Mtouches: Assistant.html

Today

"Lose this client / Utilization −10% / Renewal +5%" each render a hardcoded table. An executive can't ask "what if utilization drops 15%?" or "what if we lose our top two clients?" — the lever is fixed, so it isn't really simulation.

Proposed solution

Make it parametric: replace fixed buttons with sliders (churn %, utilization delta, price change, headcount) that recompute impact live off AKData (ARR, margin, bench cost). "Save to board pack" should produce a real artifact (see G7). This turns a static panel into the firm's planning sandbox.

Evidence: Assistant.html scenarios[] array with literal rows; runScenario(i) only swaps which pre-written block shows.

03 The system only knows "now" Gaps 6–7 · no time axis

Executive work is fundamentally about what's coming — renewals, milestones, SLA windows, contract expiries, close dates. Akashic has all of this data (expiry dates, deadlines, milestone counts) but presents it only as scattered "today" snapshots. There is no calendar, no horizon, and the morning brief that the whole home page is built around is never actually delivered anywhere.

G6
No forward horizon — renewals, milestones and SLA windows aren't laid out in time
Highfeatureeffort · Mnew: Horizon view

Today

Expiry and deadline data lives in data.js (expiry:'22 Jun 2026', daysToDeadline) but is surfaced one entity at a time. There is no single view that answers "what's hitting me over the next 90 days?" — the executive has to assemble the timeline in their head.

Proposed solution

Add a Horizon surface — a time-ordered runway of every dated obligation:

  • Renewals, milestone billings, SLA review points, contract expiries, bench-aging cliffs, on one axis.
  • Persona-filtered (CFO sees billing/close dates; DH sees delivery milestones; CEO sees renewals/board dates).
  • Threshold-crossing forecasts ("DSO breaches 60d in ~2 weeks at current trajectory").
Evidence: dated fields exist across clients, projects, contracts, invoices in data.js; no screen aggregates them on a time axis. "Renewals This Quarter" rail is a list, not a horizon.
G7
The morning brief is never delivered — "Send me the brief" is a toast
Highworkfloweffort · S–Mtouches: Home · shell.js

Today

The home page is literally an "Intelligence Feed" with a "Send me the brief" button — but it just toasts "Briefing exported to your inbox." There's no scheduled digest, no inbox/Slack delivery, no board-pack export. The executive must come to Akashic; it never comes to them.

Proposed solution

Build a real briefing & digest engine:

  • Scheduled persona-shaped digest (email/Slack) — the brief that already renders, delivered daily/weekly.
  • One-click board-pack / PDF export of the brief + scenarios + composite (ties into "Save to board pack").
  • "Brief me on X" generates a shareable snapshot of any entity.
Evidence: Home.html — onclick="AK.toast('Briefing exported to your inbox')"; Assistant.html "Save to board pack" also only toasts. Phase 1 plan flagged "CEO Board Pack auto-generator" as out of scope.

04 Three seats, not an organisation Gap 8 · the persona model doesn't scale

The persona system is the product's best idea and its tightest constraint. There are exactly three roles, one hardcoded human each, and the data is firm-wide for all of them. A real services firm runs on account owners, practice heads, project leads, HR and sales — the data already names them (Priya Nair owns Meridian; leads are assigned per project) but they have no seat, no scoped view, and no way to receive delegated work.

G8
Rigid persona model — no scoped roles, no "my portfolio", no delegation or RACI
Highfunctioneffort · Ltouches: shell.js · data model

Today

PERSONA_USER hardcodes three people; PILLAR_CONFIG gives each a fixed lens. Everyone sees the entire portfolio. There is no account-owner view (Priya can't see "my 6 accounts"), no practice-head P&L scoping, no project-lead workspace, and handoffs can only target the other two executives — never the owner who actually does the work.

Proposed solution

Generalise persona into role + scope:

  • Add functional roles (Account Owner, Practice Head, Project Lead, HR) with their own lens configs.
  • Data scoping: "my accounts / my projects / my practice" filters driven by the owner fields already in data.js.
  • Delegation + a lightweight RACI on every signal so handoff can reach the right doer, not just a peer.
Evidence: shell.js PERSONAS/PERSONA_USER = 3 fixed; data.js already carries owner/ownerInit on clients and lead on projects — the scoping data exists but is unused for access. Phase 1 plan: "only one user per persona today."

05 Executives decide alone Gap 9 · no collaboration layer

The handoff protocol is a good start, but it's a one-way throw. Real executive decisions are conversations — threads, @mentions, "what did we decide and why," who else is looking at this account right now. Akashic has none of that connective tissue; even the "Reply to Arjun" button is a toast.

G9
No shared workspace — handoff is one-directional, replies are stubs, decisions leave no shared record
Highworkfloweffort · Mtouches: shell.js · Assistant · profiles

Today

A handoff drops a signal into a peer's feed; the receiver's "Reply" just toasts. There are no comment threads on entities, no @mentions, no presence ("Devika is also viewing Meridian"), and no durable decision record. The two-way conversation the Phase 2 plan sketched is still shallow.

Proposed solution

Add a thin collaboration layer:

  • Threaded comments + @mentions on any entity, signal, or approval.
  • Two-way handoff (reply actually posts back, with notification).
  • A Decision Record on each entity timeline: what was decided, by whom, why, and the outcome (links to G1).
  • Presence/awareness so executives don't duplicate or contradict each other.
Evidence: shell.js handoff stores a record but the receiver path (Assistant.html) only renders a banner; "Reply to Arjun" = AK.toast(). No comment/thread primitive anywhere.

06 Built for a desk executives aren't at Gap 10 · no mobile / push

The personas are described as people who do "5-minute dashboard checks between meetings." That is a phone behaviour. Yet mobile is explicitly out of scope and there is no push/notification channel — so the moment that matters most (a critical signal while the CEO is in a board meeting) is the moment Akashic is unreachable.

G10
No mobile, glanceable, or push experience for between-meeting executives
Highfeatureeffort · M–Ltouches: responsive · digest

Today

Layouts are desktop grids with sticky rails and fixed widths; below ~1080px columns collapse but the experience isn't designed for a phone. Notifications live only inside the app — there's no push to where an executive actually is.

Proposed solution

Ship a glanceable mobile surface:

  • Phone-shaped brief: top 3 signals, approve/route/snooze inline, the day's horizon.
  • Push / Slack / email channel for critical + handoff notifications (pairs with G7's digest engine).
  • Approve-from-phone for the approvals queue.
Evidence: Phase 1 plan lists "Mobile / phone-sized layout" and "Real notifications backend" as out of scope; CSS uses fixed --sidebar-w and grid-template-columns tuned for desktop.

07 Trust only flows in the reading direction Gaps 11–12 · the trust layer stops at the action boundary

Akashic is meticulous about provenance when you read a number — confidence scores, source citations, a validation queue. But the moment you act, that rigor disappears. You can raise an invoice off a Tally feed that's 42 minutes stale, or act on a clause extracted at 0.71 confidence, with no warning. And the sync engine can only succeed — there is no concept of an integration being down, a write-back failing, or two systems disagreeing.

G11
No data-freshness or confidence gating on actions
Highfunctioneffort · S–Mtouches: actions.js · shell.js

Today

The sidebar already shows Tally lagging (42 min) and documents at 0.71 confidence — but the action composer ignores both. You can commit a financial write-back built on stale or low-confidence source data with no interlock. The trust layer protects reading but not deciding.

Proposed solution

Extend trust into the action path:

  • Freshness gate: warn/block when an action depends on a lagging integration ("Tally synced 42m ago — refresh before raising invoice?").
  • Confidence interlock: actions built on sub-threshold documents prompt "validate source first" (routes to Documents queue).
  • Stamp each action with the data-vintage it was based on.
Evidence: shell.js INTEGRATIONS marks Tally status:'lag'; data.js documents carry conf + validated:false; actions.js compose() reads neither before submit.
G12
No integration-failure, conflict, or reconciliation handling — sync can only succeed
Highworkfloweffort · Mnew: Integration health / exceptions

Today

simulateSync() always walks four green steps to success. There's no failed write-back, no retry, no "Salesforce rejected this," and no reconciliation when SAP and Tally disagree on an amount. For a system whose entire value is being the source of truth across five systems, the unhappy path is missing.

Proposed solution

Build an Integration Health & Exceptions workspace:

  • Sync failure states with retry / manual-resolve; failed actions surface in Approvals.
  • A reconciliation queue for cross-system data conflicts (the real EIS job-to-be-done).
  • Connection-down banners and degraded-mode behaviour, not just "lag" dots.
Evidence: actions.js simulateSync() has only a success path; Approvals "In-Flight" progress only increases. No error/conflict model in data.js.

08 The Spine has missing vertebrae Gaps 13–14 · orphaned nodes & uneven depth

The Enterprise Spine diagram on the home page promises seven connected nodes: Account → Contract → Pursuit → Project → Employee → Financial → Skill. But two of those nodes — Pursuit and Skill — have no screen behind them, even though pipeline coverage and skill-gap are headline pulse metrics. And among the nodes that do exist, depth is wildly uneven: Projects has three full persona lenses while Clients is a flat registry.

G13
Pursuit (pipeline) and Skill (capability) are on the Spine but have no pillar behind them
Highfeatureeffort · Lnew: Pipeline + Skills pillars

Today

The Spine renders a "Pursuit" node and a "Skill" node, and the CEO/DH pulse tiles show "Pipeline Coverage 2.4×" and "Skill-Pipeline Gap" — but clicking leads nowhere; there is no pipeline/sales pillar and no skills inventory. For a services firm, bench-to-pipeline matching and capability planning are core, not optional. The Phase 1 plan deferred the DH Resource-Planning workspace that would house this.

Proposed solution

  • Pipeline / Pursuit pillar: pursuits by stage/value/probability, feeding the coverage metric and renewal SOWs.
  • Skills & Capability pillar: skills inventory, gaps vs. 90-day pipeline, certifications, and the long-promised bench→pipeline matcher (data already hints at it: "6 engineers match 2 pursuits").
Evidence: Home.html spine-visual includes Pursuit & Skill nodes; pulse references "Pipeline Coverage" / "Skill-Pipeline Gap"; no Pursuits.html / Skills.html exist. Phase 1: "DH Resource Planning" deferred.
G14
Pillar depth is asymmetric — Projects has 3 persona lenses, Clients/Employees are single-lens
Mediumfeatureeffort · Mtouches: Clients · Employees

Today

Projects beautifully renders three role-specific views (DH health board, CEO bubble map, CFO P&L grid). Clients is a single registry; Employees a single roster; Financials hard-blocks the DH entirely. The persona promise is only fully kept on one pillar.

Proposed solution

Bring the other 3-persona pillars to parity using the Projects pattern. Highest payoff: Clients (CEO portfolio/concentration vs. CFO receivables vs. owner account view) and Employees (DH staffing vs. CEO talent-risk vs. HR development). Give DH a scoped Financials slice rather than a hard 🔒.

Evidence: Projects.html has proj-root rendering 3 persona templates; Clients.html/Employees.html render one; Financials.html shows an "Access Restricted" wall for DH.

09 There is no control plane Gap 15 · everything is hardcoded

Every policy in the product is baked into source: the 30% concentration threshold, the ≤52-day DSO target, the 99.5% SLA bar, who approves what, who can see what. There is no settings, admin, or governance layer — so the product can't be configured for a different firm, and access control is a single hard wall.

G15
No admin / governance layer — thresholds, alert rules, approval authority and RBAC are all hardcoded
Highfunctioneffort · Mnew: Settings / Admin

Today

Thresholds are literals in feed/pulse code. Notification routing is a static per-persona array. There's no snooze/mute, no escalation rules, no approval-authority limits (who can sign off >₹5 Cr), and RBAC is a binary 🔒 page-block. The audit log records actions but there's no access log of who viewed sensitive comp/financial data.

Proposed solution

Add a control plane:

  • Policy & thresholds editor (concentration, DSO, SLA, margin floors) feeding the signal engine.
  • Alert routing & escalation rules; per-user notification prefs; snooze/mute.
  • Granular RBAC + approval-authority matrix (replace the hard wall); view-level access audit.
Evidence: thresholds inline in Home.html pulse/feed; shell.js PERSONA_NOTIFS is a static array, "Mark all read" is the only control; Financials.html/Collections.html use full-page 🔒 blocks; audit log is action-only.

10 No outside-in context, and no memory Gaps 16–17 · depth & personalization

Two quieter but compounding gaps: every number is internal and vs-plan (no idea whether 22.4% margin is good for the peer set), and the system has no memory of the individual using it — the feed, search, and priorities are identical on every visit, regardless of what you dismissed or acted on yesterday.

G16
No benchmarking or outside-in context — all metrics are internal and vs-plan only
Mediumfeatureeffort · Mdata partnership

Today

Pulse tiles compare only to internal plan ("1.6pt below plan"). An executive can't tell whether the firm's DSO, margin, utilization or rev/employee are strong or weak relative to the industry. Strategic decisions need an outside-in reference.

Proposed solution

Add benchmark overlays — peer / industry bands on key pulse metrics ("DSO 58d vs. IT-services median 49d"), sourced from a data partner or anonymised cohort. Even a static reference set materially sharpens the CEO and CFO narratives.

Evidence: PULSE_CONFIG tiles carry only vst (vs-plan) strings; no external/peer reference anywhere in the model.
G17
No memory or personalization — dismiss/snooze don't persist; search is a static list
Mediumfunctioneffort · S–Mtouches: Home · shell.js

Today

"Flag" and "Assign" on feed cards only toast; nothing is remembered between visits. The feed is identical every load. The ⌘K palette is a hardcoded list that doesn't actually search the data model, and there are no recents or saved views.

Proposed solution

  • Persist dismiss / snooze / flag per user; learn which signal types a user repeatedly ignores and de-prioritise them.
  • Make ⌘K a real search over AKData with recents and saved views/filters.
  • "You snoozed this yesterday" / "resolved since your last visit" continuity cues.
Evidence: Home.html quick actions = AK.toast(); shell.js PALETTE_ITEMS is a fixed array filtered by label only; no per-user state store beyond persona.

The one-line thesis

Akashic has already built the hard part — a connected, persona-aware, citation-backed view of the firm. The gaps are not in the seeing; they're in the acting, learning, scheduling, scaling and conversing around what's seen. Close the loop (G1–G3), make the named feature real (G4), and put the firm on a time axis (G6–G7), and Akashic moves from an exceptional executive dashboard to a true executive operating system.
