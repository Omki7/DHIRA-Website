Design Proposal · v1 · 04 June 2026

Persona-aware experience — making roles structural, not cosmetic.

Today, the persona switcher only changes the landing page. A real Executive Intelligence System has to do much more than that — what an executive sees, what gets prioritized, and what they can do all need to shift based on who they are. Below is the plan to make that real.

01 The problem we're solving

The three personas — CEO Arjun Mehta, CFO Devika Rao, Delivery Head Karthik Menon — run very different operating loops. They face different questions in the morning, take different actions during the day, and are accountable for different outcomes at quarter-end. Yet the current product gives them an identical experience apart from one redirect.

The result: every executive has to mentally filter out 60–70% of what they're looking at to find the 20–30% that matters. That's the exact opposite of executive intelligence.

The fix is not to build three different products. It's to make one product structurally aware of role across seven concrete dimensions, and let the same data and same screens render through the right lens.

04 The Cross-Persona Handoff Protocol

The product blueprint calls this out as non-negotiable, and it's what makes role-awareness more than just filtering: any signal can be routed from one executive to another, with context preserved.

Concretely, every pressure-point card and every composite alert will get a ↗ Route to… control. Selecting another persona opens a one-line note, then drops the alert into their queue with an "assigned by Arjun" provenance pill. The original card shows a handed off state so the sender knows it's no longer their problem to track.

The receiver sees it in their notifications with a distinct icon and a "see what Arjun saw" link that opens the exact card with the same data context the sender had.

05 Scope

In scope · this round

  • Sidebar reorder & primary-pillar emphasis per persona
  • Pulse tile set swapped per persona on Home
  • AI brief rewritten per persona (extended — context-aware, not just name swap)
  • Pressure feed default filter per persona, sort priority per persona
  • Right rail content swapped per persona (3 rails + This Week)
  • Decisions bar primary action set per persona
  • Notifications filtered & re-sorted per persona
  • Approvals page default tab per persona
  • Cross-Persona Handoff control on every pressure card
  • Subtle persona color accent on the active sidebar item to make role visible at a glance

Out of scope · noted for later

  • Dedicated CFO Collections workspace screen
  • Dedicated DH Resource Planning screen (bench → pipeline match)
  • Dedicated CEO Board Pack auto-generator
  • Persona-scoped data ("my accounts" filter — only one user per persona today)
  • Mobile / phone-sized layout
  • Real notifications backend (currently mocked)

07 What I want you to confirm

Three decisions before I implement

1
Hide pillars vs collapse them. Today CFO hides Employees and DH hides Financials. I'd rather keep all pillars visible but de-emphasized — moved below a "More" divider — so the system-of-execution philosophy ("everything is connected") stays visible. OK to switch from hide → collapse-below-fold?
2
CFO's home. Today CFO lands on Financials.html. I can either keep that and make Financials persona-shaped, or send CFO to Home.html with a CFO-shaped Intelligence Feed (pulse, brief, feed, rails all swapped). The second option means one "Home" surface for all roles which is conceptually cleaner. Prefer single Home for all, or persona-specific landing screens?
3
Cross-persona handoff fidelity. Minimum-viable is a Route to… control that toasts + marks the sender card as handed off. A richer version actually shows the handoff in the receiver's notification panel with a "view as Arjun" affordance. MVP or richer? Richer is ~30% more work and a clearer demo.

Once you confirm the three decisions above (or just say "go with your recommendation"), I'll implement top-to-bottom and show you the result.
