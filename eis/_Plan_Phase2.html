Today the home page swaps pulse tiles, AI brief, model panel, and right rail per persona — but the structure is identical across all three. CEO gets a Spine diagram + composite card; CFO and DH get the same Spine diagram + composite card even though those modules are CEO-tuned. What each homepage becomes

04
Deepen one pillar page with real persona views — Projects
All four pillar pages today do at most superficial persona masking (e.g. RLS-restricted columns). The pillar that has the most to gain from full persona-awareness is Projects, because all three roles legitimately care about it but for completely different reasons.

Why Projects (and not Clients / Employees / Financials)

  • Clients — CEO-anchored; CFO's view is "what they owe me" (already on Financials), DH's view is restricted.
  • Employees — DH and CEO want similar but different views; CFO is mostly restricted.
  • Financials — already CFO-shaped end to end. DH is blocked. CEO view is similar to CFO.
  • Projects — all three personas have legitimate, distinct lenses. Strongest persona payoff.

The three Projects views

LensWhat you see firstWhat you can do
CEO Strategic project map: 31 projects by client × revenue × margin bubble. Concentration overlay. Red bubbles = renewal-critical. Escalate · Open client 360 · Add to board pack
CFO Project P&L grid: 31 projects sorted by margin compression. Booked / billed / collected / unbilled-WIP per project. Pink rows = leaking cash. Raise invoice · Approve write-off · Re-forecast revenue
DH Delivery health board: 31 projects as kanban columns (Green / Amber / Red / Recovery). Each card shows lead + days late + utilisation. Approve CR · Redeploy · Re-baseline · Open project

The data is the same; the layout, sort, and action verbs are different. Same toggle as on Home — flip persona and the page rerenders.

05
Build a new dedicated workspace
The Phase 1 plan called out three candidate workspaces: CFO Collections, DH Resource Planning, CEO Board Pack. You've narrowed it to the first two. They're roughly equal effort (~5-6 hours each). Below is what each looks like and which one I'd build first.

CFO Collections Workspace

Why this first. The current Financials page surfaces aging buckets but Devika still can't actually do collections from inside the product — she'd jump to CRM or email. A workspace turns ₹2.4 Cr of stuck cash into a tracked workflow. It's also the persona view that most reflects the brand promise — "every figure one click from action."

Workspace structure

  • Aging buckets as switchable columns (kanban-style) — 0-30 / 31-60 / 61-90 / 90+
  • Each invoice card: client + amount + days + last-touch + risk score
  • Action drawer: send reminder · escalate to legal · log call · adjust terms · write-off
  • Auto-suggest playbook: "Northwind hasn't responded in 21 days — recommend escalation"
  • Daily ₹ unlocked counter at top — gamifies progress, ties to Home pulse
  • Connected to handoff: routed-from-Arjun items show with provenance pill

DH Resource Planning

Why second. Equally valuable but conceptually closer to what Projects + Employees already do; Collections has no current analogue in the product. Could ship in Phase 3.

If we did it instead

  • Bench → Pipeline matcher: 6 idle engineers vs 2 incoming pursuits
  • Heat map: utilisation by pod × week, drag-drop redeploys
  • SPOF list with succession-plan drafting
  • Skill-gap projection vs 90-day pipeline

My recommendation: CFO Collections workspace this round. If you'd rather do DH Resource Planning first, say so and I'll flip.
