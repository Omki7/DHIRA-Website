## 1. Company & Brand Profile

**DHIRA** is an AI-native data intelligence company building **Akashic**, a unified data, knowledge, and agentic AI platform. Model: Advise, Engineer, Solutionize.

- DHIRA = the company. Akashic = the platform. Keep distinct, like Palantir/Foundry, Databricks/Lakebase, Microsoft/Fabric.
- Akashic unifies structured, unstructured, and streaming data into one governed foundation, links it via a knowledge graph, and runs BI, conversational AI, ML, and agentic orchestration on top.
- Proof points: national-scale Digital Public Infrastructure delivery in India; Maha Hackathon 2025 and Telangana AI Rising 2025 winner; Startup India recognized; MSME and GeM registered; offices in New York, Hyderabad, Bangalore.
- Positioning: a platform with a delivery team behind it, one accountable partner, not a stack of vendors. Differs from pure SaaS (Hex, Cube, ThoughtSpot, Sisense) by having a services layer, and from pure consultancies by having a shipped product.
- Closest analogs: Palantir (government/knowledge graph), Cube.dev (semantic layer/grounded messaging), Hex (confident brand voice).
- Sectors: Government, Smart Cities, Healthcare, Banking and Finance, Retail and Manufacturing, Education, Energy and Utility.

## 2. Brand Voice Rules

- Direct, declarative, short. Headlines are complete thoughts, not stubs.
- No em dashes in marketing copy. Use colons or periods.
- No invented hyphenation; standard forms only (Real-Time, on-premises, 72-hour).
- Precise over vague. No "powerful," "seamless," "next-generation" as filler.
- No vaporware. Never market a roadmap feature as live today.
- Claims must be real. No invented SLA numbers, certifications, or client names.
- Government clients that can't be named publicly use descriptive attribution ("Senior Official, State Government"), never fabricated names.
- Two-tier motif: "trust / trace / act on" = the emotional promise. "Grounded" = the technical mechanism (knowledge graph linking structured + unstructured data). Keep them distinct, don't collapse into one repeated word.

## 3. Essential Design Tokens

**Philosophy:** Clarity over decoration. Density without clutter. Tonal layers, not heavy shadows. Calm, precise, efficient. Minimal, corporate-modern, AI-native.

**Core colors:**
- Background: `#FAFAFB` · Cards/surfaces: `#FFFFFF` · Primary text: `#1A1C1D` · Secondary text: `#45474A`
- Primary action (buttons): `#1C1D1F`, hover `#2F3132`
- Accent (links, AI highlights, focus): `#3E63DD`, subtle bg `#EEF1FC`
- Borders/dividers: `#EEEFF1`
- Status: success `#30A46C`, warning `#F5D90A`, error `#E5484D`
- *(Swap accent-blue for DHIRA's own brand color if/when supplied.)*

**Typography:** Inter only. Hero headline ~56-64px/600 weight. Section headlines ~32-36px/600. Body text 14-16px/400, max line length ~65 characters. Tabular numbers for all data/tables.

**Spacing:** 4px/8px grid baseline. Card padding 16-24px. Section spacing 64-96px between major homepage sections.

**Shape:** Rounded corners throughout - 8px default for buttons/cards/inputs, 16-24px for hero/feature cards, full-round for pills/badges/avatars. No sharp corners.

**Components, quick reference:**
- Buttons: 40px height, 8px radius, dark fill primary / white-bordered secondary, verb-first labels.
- Cards: white background, subtle border, no shadow unless floating.
- Tables: tabular numbers, right-aligned, light gray header row.
- Charts: minimal grid lines, accent-blue for highlighted series.

**Motion:** Fast and functional, never decorative. ~150-250ms standard transitions, ease-out for entrances. Respect reduced-motion preferences.

**Accessibility:** WCAG AA contrast minimum (4.5:1 normal text). Visible focus rings. Proper heading hierarchy.
